Changing Your Mindset to Improve Your Interviewing Skills: The Key to Interview Success

Author: Frendy Eka Styabudi


The job interview is a crucial stage in the job search process. This is where you have the opportunity to showcase your talents, qualifications and fit with the company. However, many people feel nervous and insecure during interviews. This can hinder their performance and lead to failure.


Fortunately, by changing your mindset and implementing the right strategies, you can improve your interviewing skills and open up opportunities to land the job of your dreams. Here are some tips to help you out:

                       
1. Believe in Yourself and Your Abilities
                        
The first step is to believe in yourself and your abilities. Remember that you have a lot to offer an employer. Believe in your knowledge, experience and talent. This positive attitude will shine through in the interview and make you more attractive to the interviewer.
                        
2. Do Thorough Research and Preparation
                        
    Before the interview, do in-depth research on the company and the position you are applying for. Learn about their vision, mission, values, and products or services. Understand what they look for in an ideal candidate. Prepare answers to common interview questions, such as “Tell me about yourself” or “What do you know about this company?”. The more prepared you are, the more confident you will be in answering questions and showing your enthusiasm for the position.

3. Practice Interview Answers and Techniques
                        
    Practice answering interview questions verbally and in front of a mirror. Enlist the help of friends or family to conduct a mock interview session. Pay attention to your body language, voice intonation and eye contact. Make sure you speak clearly, concisely and in a structured manner. Practice answering questions in a straightforward manner and focus on the key points.

4. Manage Nervousness with Relaxation Techniques
                        
    It's normal to feel nervous before an interview. However, don't let your nerves control you. Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or meditation to calm your mind and body. Visualize yourself answering the questions successfully and getting a positive outcome.

5. Show Enthusiasm and Genuine Interest
                        
    Interviewers want to see that you are genuinely interested in the company and the position. Show your enthusiasm by asking intelligent questions about the company, work culture and development opportunities. Show that you have done your research and understand what the company has to offer.

6. Stay Polite and Professional
                        
    Always maintain a polite and professional demeanor during the interview. Dress neatly and in accordance with the company culture. Arrive on time and greet the interviewer in a friendly manner. Show respect and appreciation to the interviewer and the company.

7. Be Positive and Grateful
                        
    Even if you don't get the job, keep a positive and professional attitude. Thank the interviewer for their time and express your appreciation for the opportunity. Show that you are still interested in the company and hope to have the opportunity to work there in the future.

Remember that an interview is a two-way process. Not only are you being interviewed, but you also have the right to assess whether the company and the position are a good fit for you. Use this opportunity to learn more about the company and make sure that you want to work there.

By changing your mindset, doing your research and preparation, and applying the right strategies, you can improve your interviewing skills and open up opportunities to land the job of your dreams. Believe in yourself, showcase your talents and abilities, and be positive and professional.

Remember that the key to interview success is preparation, practice and confidence.
